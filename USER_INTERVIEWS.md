# User Interviews — SpendLens

Insights gathered from cold DMs and local network outreach to 3 key personas.

---

## Interview 1: A.K.
**Role:** Founder
**Company Stage:** Seed (Team of 5)
**Key Quotes:**
- *"I have no idea how much I'm spending on AI. I just pay the invoices as they come."*
- *"The biggest fear isn't the $20 seat, it's the $2,000 accidental OpenAI API bill."*
- *"If you could tell me exactly which engineer has an idle Copilot seat, I'd pay for that."*

**Most Surprising Thing:**
A.K. wasn't worried about the *average* monthly cost, but the *variance*. They wanted a "shock absorber" or an alert system more than just a one-time audit.

**What it changed:**
I added the "OpenAI API usage" tool to the audit form. Initially, I was only focused on monthly subscription seats, but for founders, the API credit burn is the "silent killer."

---

## Interview 2: J.M.
**Role:** Engineering Manager
**Company Stage:** Series B (Team of 40)
**Key Quotes:**
- *"We have a Cursor vs. Copilot war every week. Half the team wants one, half wants the other."*
- *"Procurement takes 3 weeks. If I can show the CFO we save $500/mo by switching, I can get new tools approved instantly."*
- *"The shareable link needs to look 'corporate'—no memes, just hard data."*

**Most Surprising Thing:**
J.M. views SpendLens as a **weapon for internal politics**. They use the data to justify tool switches to the finance department.

**What it changed:**
I shifted the design aesthetic to be more "premium/editorial" (Zinc/Emerald) instead of a "fun startup" look. It needed to look authoritative enough for a CFO to trust.

---

## Interview 3: S.P.
**Role:** Indie Hacker
**Company Stage:** Bootstrapped (Solo)
**Key Quotes:**
- *"I'm a cheapskate. I want to know the absolute minimum I can pay to get the job done."*
- *"I use Perplexity for search but also pay for ChatGPT Plus. Is that redundant?"*
- *"Your 'Start your own audit' link is actually what I'd share on my newsletter."*

**Most Surprising Thing:**
Solo founders are the most likely to share their results publicly to "humble brag" about how lean they are.

**What it changed:**
I implemented the clipboard sharing with the checkmark feedback specifically for this "viral sharing" behavior. I also added the "already optimal" message for users with zero savings to keep them engaged.
