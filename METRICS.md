# Performance Metrics — SpendLens

## The North Star Metric
**"Validated High-Savings Leads"**
- **Definition:** An audit completed where total monthly savings are > $100 AND the user has submitted their email address.
- **Why:** DAU or total audits are vanity metrics if they don't lead to potential Credex customers. A high-savings lead is the primary indicator of SpendLens' value as a business engine.

## 3 Input Metrics
1. **Viral Coefficient (K-factor):** The number of new audits generated by a shareable `/audit/[id]` link.
2. **Form Completion Rate:** % of users who start the first input field and reach the results page.
3. **Credex CTA Click-through Rate:** % of users on the results page who click "Book a consultation" or the Credex link.

## What to Instrument First
- **PostHog / Mixpanel Events:**
    - `audit_started`: Triggered on first input change.
    - `audit_completed`: Triggered on results page mount.
    - `lead_captured`: Triggered on email form submission.
    - `share_button_clicked`: Tracking the intent to go viral.
- **Vercel Web Analytics:** To monitor page load speeds and geographic distribution of users.

## The Pivot Trigger
**"If Lead Capture Rate is < 5% after 500 audits."**
- **Reasoning:** If people are getting their results but refuse to leave an email to "save" them or stay updated, it means the perceived value of the audit isn't high enough, or the trust barrier is too high.
- **The Pivot:** Instead of a free-to-results flow, move to a "gated" results flow where the basic stats are free, but the "How to save" breakdown requires an email.
